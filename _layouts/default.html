Broadly, I am interested in the neural mechanisms underlying motivational states - specifically the cellular and molecular processes that induce, maintain, and terminate adaptive behavioral and physiological responses to sensory stimuli. Currently, in the lab of Dr. Gord Fishell, I study how threat-related information is represented and structured in lateral septal cells and circuits, and how molecular and functional organization therein gives rise to adaptive defensive behavior.

Prior to starting my graduate training, I worked in the labs of Drs. Philip V. Holmes, Michael Bruchas, and Paul Greengard. In these labs, I used an array of behavioral, pharmacological, optogenetic, and molecular approaches to examine the neurobiological mechanisms underlying threat responsivity, feeding, and behavioral stress.

Outside of the lab, I enjoy climbing, exploring mountains, biking, playing/writing music, and thinking about the philosophy of emotions and how to study it in the laboratory.
