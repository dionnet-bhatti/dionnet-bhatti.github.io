In Dr. Todd Anthony's laboratory at Harvard Medical School/Boston Children's Hospital, I am investigatiing how experience and perception shape neural dynamics and concomitant defensive behaviors using optical tools for monitoring and manipulating neural activity.

Prior to starting PhD training, I worked in the labs of Drs. Philip V. Holmes, Michael Bruchas, and Paul Greengard. In these labs, I used behavioral pharmacology, optogenetics, and mouse genetics to investigate the cellular, molecular, and neuromodulatory mechanisms underlying threat reactivity, feeding, and behavioral stress responses.

The brain governs what we do, how we feel, and ultimately makes us who we are. Broadly, I am interested in how this complex system of cells allows us to evaluate our experiences, adapt, and direct behaviors necessary for survival. My research aims to understand the neural circuit, cellular, and molecular mechanisms that allow us to perform motivated behaviors such as seeking pleasure or avoiding pain and how previous experiences regulate behavior both adaptively and maladaptively.
