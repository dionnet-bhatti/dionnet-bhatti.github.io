I am a PhD student in Harvard Universityâ€™s Program in Neuroscience. Broadly, I am interested in how neural systems evaluate our experiences, adapt, and direct behaviors necessary for survival. In Dr. Todd Anthony's laboratory at Harvard Medical School/Boston Children's Hospital, I use optical tools for monitoring and manipulating neural activity to investigate how experience and perception shape neural dynamics and concomitant defensive behaviors.

Prior to starting my graduate training, I worked in the labs of Drs. Philip V. Holmes, Michael Bruchas, and Paul Greengard. In these labs, I used behavioral, pharmacological, optogenetic, and molecular-genetic approaches to investigate the cellular, molecular, and neuromodulatory mechanisms underlying threat reactivity, feeding, and behavioral stress responses.
